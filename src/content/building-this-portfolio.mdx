## Building This Portfolio

Or: what happens when you finally run out of excuses.

I did not have a portfolio for a long time. Most of my friends did. I kept telling myself I did not have enough to show — not enough projects, nothing polished enough, nothing worth putting out there. That was partly true and mostly just avoidance.

Then I had two months of vacation, needed an internship, and the excuse stopped working. So I built one.

## Why from scratch

I could have used a template. I chose not to — not because I think it makes me a better developer, but because I knew I would learn more by making the decisions myself. What goes on the hero section. How to handle routing. Whether to have a blog at all. Templates answer those questions for you, which is convenient and also the entire problem.

The stack ended up being TanStack Router, Vite, Tailwind v4, and Motion for animations. Blog posts are MDX. Everything runs on Bun. None of this was particularly novel for me — I had used most of it before. The two things I actually had not touched were MDX and building a `Cmd+K` command palette. Both are now things I understand, which is the point.

I chose TanStack Router over Next.js because Next.js is more than I needed. I wanted a static site with type-safe routing. TanStack gives me that without the overhead. Tailwind v4 is cleaner to set up than v3. Motion let me think about what to animate instead of how to write the CSS for it. These were not hard choices — they were just honest ones.

## What I got wrong

The hero section is too bare. I know that. Minimalism was the theme, but there is a version of minimalism that is intentional and a version that is just… not finishing the thing. Mine might be the second one.

Setting up MDX took longer than I expected — not because it is complex, but because I had never done it. Getting the rendering pipeline right, overriding components for code blocks and blockquotes, figuring out how file globbing worked in this context — it was unfamiliar territory. That kind of friction is annoying in the moment and useful in hindsight.

The library page took the most thinking. I read a decent amount — CS, psychology, fiction — and I was not sure what shape it should take. Bookshelf? Just books? Papers too? I landed on "library" because it can hold anything. It is still sparse. I have bigger ideas for it. They did not make it into V1.

## The things I actually care about

The Today I Learned section is the one I am most attached to. The idea is simple: when I learn something or figure out a tricky problem, I write it down. Not for anyone else. For the version of me who needs to think more carefully before moving on. It is easy to solve something and immediately forget you solved it. This is a forcing function against that.

The "What I Did Not Finish" section I borrowed from someone else's portfolio. I liked the honesty of it. Most side projects do not get completed. That is just how it goes. But unfinished things still taught you something, and pretending they do not exist felt like the wrong move.

## Did I use AI?

Yes. Boilerplate, the simple parts, moving faster on things that did not require my judgment. I finished this in a week partly because of that. I do not think that is something to apologize for.

A V1. A starting point. A snapshot of where I am right now — final year, two months of free time, a growing list of things I want to build, and a lot I still do not know.
